Origin Story
"I drove 2 hours to Rumney. The rock was wet. The weather app said 0% rain."

That's it. That's the whole story. I'm Frank — I climb outdoors in New England, which means I play a constant gamble with microclimate weather. Standard forecast apps don't know what granite friction feels like after morning fog. They don't know that north-facing walls seep for days after rain. Climbers know. But there was no app that asked them.

So I built one. CodClimb uses the Open-Meteo API to pull hyper-local weather data for any crag, then runs it through a climbing-specific scoring algorithm — accounting for humidity, dew point, recent precipitation, wind, and temperature — to output a single number: your Climb Score. 0 to 100.

Then the community layer kicks in. Real climbers post real-time reports. They earn reputation with every post. That's the data weather APIs will never have.

The Problem

Weather apps give city-level forecasts. Crags are in canyons, on cliff faces, in forests. They dry differently. They seep differently. A 0% rain forecast means nothing when the rock is seeping from two days ago.

The Solution

A climbing-specific weather score + live community reports. Data where it matters: the actual wall, the actual moment, from people who are actually there.

The Market

7.7M climbers in the US. Most climb outdoors at some point. There is no dominant app solving the "is my crag climbable right now?" problem with community data. Not yet.

Features

Everything you need before you leave the car

🌡️
Weather Intelligence Score

A 0–100 score built specifically for climbing — weighing humidity, dew point, wind, temperature, and precipitation probability in a formula that matches real friction conditions, not just comfort.

🧗
Live Community Reports

Climbers post condition updates from the crag — specific walls, specific routes. Wet rock? Dry rock? Seeping? Someone who's there right now can tell you. Filter by route, sector, or time.

📸
Photo Reporting

Attach a photo to your condition report. A wet seam, a dry hold, a sunrise on the cliff face. Visual proof beats text any day. Build the visual history of your local crag.

🏅
Reputation System

The more you report, the higher your rank. From Chalk Bag to Crag Legend. Community trust is earned in the field. Your reputation is visible to other climbers, so quality reports get respected.

📅
48-Hour Forecast View

Plan your next two days at a glance. Hourly Climb Scores let you pick the perfect window — whether you want morning conditions or an afternoon session.

📍
Crag Database

Search for any crag by name or location. Community-sourced crag profiles let climbers add local knowledge — shade/sun orientation, typical drying time, access notes.
